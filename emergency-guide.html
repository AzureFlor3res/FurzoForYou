Emergency Guide (Quick Reference)

Use this page for fast decisions. In life-threatening situations, contact emergency veterinary help immediately.

Emergency symptoms checklist

  • Not breathing or severe breathing trouble
  • Heavy bleeding that does not stop
  • Collapse, seizures, or unconsciousness
  • Suspected poisoning
  • Hit by vehicle or major trauma

Immediate response steps

  1. Stay calm and secure the area.
  2. Protect yourself from panic bites.
  3. Call emergency vet/clinic and describe symptoms.
  4. Transport safely using a towel or board if needed.

Basic first aid guidance

  • Apply gentle pressure to bleeding wounds.
  • Keep dog warm and quiet.
  • Do not give human medicine unless vet-directed.
  • For heat stress: cool gradually, not with ice shock.

When urgent help is required

If symptoms are sudden, severe, or rapidly worsening, do not wait at home. Delays can reduce survival and recovery chances.

Quick decision flow chart

Step 1: Is breathing normal and dog responsive?
No: Call emergency clinic now and start safe transport.
Yes: Check bleeding, pain, temperature, and behaviour changes.
If unsure: Call a vet helpline for immediate guidance.
Fast and calm action can save lives.
