Dog Behaviour

Understanding behaviour lowers fear, prevents bites, and makes trust-building easier.

Body language chart

SignalMeaningBest response
Loose body + soft eyesComfort, opennessApproach slowly
Tail tucked, ears backFear/anxietyGive space
Stiff posture + hard stareHigh warningBack away calmly

Why dogs react

Dogs bark, growl, hide, or freeze when excited, fearful, protective, or in pain. Warning behaviour is communication—not “bad attitude.”

Visual behaviour map

Safe approach steps

  1. Observe first.
  2. Approach from side.
  3. Let dog come first.
  4. Stop if stress signs appear.

Build trust

  • Use reward-based training.
  • Keep routines predictable.
  • Avoid punishment for fear.
  • Respect sleep and rest zones.
Read signals first. React second.
