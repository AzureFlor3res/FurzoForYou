Health & Warning Signs

Early recognition saves lives. Watch for physical and behaviour changes and respond quickly.

!Signs of illness

  • Loss of appetite for more than one day
  • Vomiting, diarrhea, or blood in stool
  • Coughing, sneezing, or breathing difficulty
  • Unusual thirst or urination changes

?Behavioural changes

Sudden withdrawal, confusion, irritability, or unusual clinginess may indicate pain, stress, or illness. Compare behaviour with your dog’s normal routine.

⚕Injury indicators

Limping, swelling, bleeding, avoiding touch, or repeated licking of one area are common injury signs. Limit movement and seek veterinary advice.

⚠Emergency warning signs

  • Collapse or unresponsiveness
  • Seizures
  • Bloated abdomen with distress
  • Heavy bleeding or trauma
  • Severe breathing difficulty

When to seek professional help

If symptoms are severe, sudden, or worsen in a few hours, contact a veterinarian immediately. Trust your instincts—if your dog feels “not normal,” check early.

Monitoring and prevention tips

  • Keep a health diary (food, stool, mood, activity).
  • Maintain parasite prevention and vaccines.
  • Provide clean water and safe environment.
  • Schedule regular vet exams and dental checks.
Awareness today can prevent serious illness tomorrow.
