Why Dog Vaccinations Matter

Vaccination is one of the most important ways to protect dogs, families, and the wider community.

Purpose of vaccination

Vaccines train the immune system to recognise harmful diseases early. This lowers the chance of severe illness and death.

How vaccines protect dogs

Vaccinated dogs are less likely to develop dangerous infections and often recover better if exposed. Puppies are especially vulnerable and need early protection.

How vaccines protect humans

Some diseases, including rabies, are zoonotic (spread between animals and humans). Vaccinating dogs protects public health and prevents avoidable tragedies.

Common preventable diseases

  • Rabies
  • Parvovirus
  • Distemper
  • Adenovirus/hepatitis
  • Leptospirosis (where recommended)

Vaccination schedule basics

Puppies usually receive a series of shots over several weeks. Adult dogs need boosters at intervals advised by a veterinarian based on local disease risk.

Regular boosters are essential

Immunity can reduce over time. Booster doses refresh protection and keep community immunity strong.

Risks of not vaccinating

Unvaccinated dogs face high risk of severe disease, expensive treatment, quarantine requirements, and possible spread to other animals and humans.

Responsible ownership role

Keep vaccine records, set reminders, and discuss concerns with a licensed vet. Vaccination is not only personal care—it is a social responsibility.

Healthy dogs, safer families, stronger communities.
